Tracking Democratic Trends in Latin America

Autocratization in the Region

Latin America Erupts: Millennial Authoritarianism in El Salvador

Journal of Democracy

President Nayib Bukele has used his legislative supermajority to dismiss top judicial figures, deepening the democratic crisis in El Salvador. The move underscores his blend of populist and authoritarian governance, raising concerns about institutional independence.

Nayib Bukele: Populism and Autocratization

Journal of Geography, Politics and Society

This article examines Bukele’s presidency as a modern example of democratic backsliding, analyzing how his leadership has eroded pluralism, civil liberties, and checks and balances in the country’s political system.

Why Do Voters Support Leaders Who Undermine Democracy?

Keough School of Global Affairs

The study explores El Salvador’s transition from authoritarianism to democracy and investigates why many citizens continue to support leaders who concentrate power and weaken democratic institutions.

Nicaragua's Farcical Election Marks Consolidation of Ortega's Autocracy

Carnegie Endowment for International Peace

President Daniel Ortega has tightened his grip on power through repression and fraudulent elections. The article highlights how Nicaragua’s democratic institutions have been hollowed out, setting a troubling precedent for the region.

Let Democracy Triumph in Haiti

Unitarian Universalist Service Committee (UUSC)

Following the assassination of President Jovenel Moïse, Haiti has faced a democratic vacuum. The unelected leadership of Prime Minister Ariel Henry and ongoing suppression of civil society signal troubling autocratic trends.

Mexico Takes Another Step Toward Its Authoritarian Past

Brookings Institution

President López Obrador’s recent actions have undermined electoral independence and centralized authority, prompting fears that Mexico is drifting away from democratic norms and back toward autocratic governance.

Democratization in the Region

Honduras Rises Up to Reclaim Its Democracy

Progressive International (Nov 2021)

After years of political violence and disinformation, Hondurans elected Xiomara Castro in 2021. This peaceful transition marks a significant democratic breakthrough following the 2009 coup.

A Chance for Real Democracy in Honduras

History News Network

This piece traces Honduras’s democratic history, focusing on the shift from externally imposed systems to grassroots civic engagement, and emphasizing the significance of recent elections.

Challenges and Opportunities in the Dominican Republic

International IDEA (April 2024)

Under President Luis Abinader, the Dominican Republic has seen improvements in rule of law and civil accountability. Reforms and active civil society participation position the country as a democratic bright spot in Latin America.

Dominicans Protest Suspended Elections to Defend Democracy

Swarthmore College Global Nonviolent Action Database

In 2020, widespread protests erupted following the suspension of municipal elections. These demonstrations led to electoral reforms and enhanced democratic transparency, showing the power of civic activism.
